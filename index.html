What Is Rickrolling?

"Rickrolling" is a form of bait-and-switch prank on the internet that involves posting a link that is supposedly relevant or uselful to the topic at hand in online forums or discussions, but re-directs the viewer to the music video of "Never Gonna Give You Up," a 1987 dance pop single by English singer-songwriter Rick Astley.


The prank became a trend sometime in spring of 2007, but a YouTuber named Erik Helwig claims to have pulled the prank on a Michigan radio station as early as August 31st, 2006.

History Of The Song

The original "Never Gonna Give You Up" was released by Rick Astley, a singer-songwriter from England, in 1987 as part of his solo debut album Whenever You Need Somebody.


The music video portion was filmed in London, around the Freston Road area.


The song was extremely popular, becoming the best-selling hit single of the year in the UK. It stayed at the top of the chart for 5 weeks in the UK, and eventually topped charts in 25 other countries, including the US and Germany. A year later, the song won the award for "Best British Single" at the 1988 Brit Awards.


Awards And Popularity

On March 12 1988, "Never Gonna Give You Up" reached number 1 in the American magazine Billiboard's Hot 100 chart, and stayed there for two weeks in a row. The song then proceeded to top the charts in 25 countries worldwide and sell over 2 million copies.



In 2008, near the beginning of the Rickrolling trend, a collective campaign by thousands of people on the internet resulted in the song winning MTV Europe's music award for "Best Act Ever". In 2023, Billboard magazine had still ranked the song among the 500 best pop songs of all time, almost 4 decades later.


The song's continued popularity is due in part to it being extremely reminiscent of 80's music and style, with Time Out magazine saying:



"Astley's weirdly robust croon and his romantic-wooing-as-used-car-salesman pitch (...) It all adds up to three-and-a-half of the most effervescent minutes in the '80s canon."



In April 2020, The Guardian ranked it a number 44 in their list of "The 100 Greatest UK number 1s". In a seperate article, The Guardian described it as "A brilliantly written song".


As of October 17, 2024, The song (as posted on Rick Astley's YouTube channel) has around 1,581,810,500 views. (Over 1.5 billion!), making it the 178th most-viewed YouTube video of all time.

Random Facts

  1. The song was reportedly played in 1989 as part of Operation Nifty Package, a psychological warfare campaign intended to convince Panamanian dictator Manuel Noriega to surrender during the United States invasion of Panama.

  2. Astley appeared in the 2008 Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade, interrupting a song performed by people on a parade float with a lipsynched performance of "Never Gonna Give You Up".

  3. A YouTuber by the name of MusRest has used AI to remake the original music video in 4k, along with many other older music videos. His 4k version can be found here.

The Rise Of Rickrolling

As was previously mentioned in this article, the prank became a trend sometime in spring of 2007, but a YouTuber named Erik Helwig claims to have pulled the prank on a Michigan radio station as early as August 31st, 2006.


According to statistics from Google Trends (Which keeps track of all google searches for a specific topic), the terms "Rick Astley" and "Rickroll" were searched most in April of 2008. Following that, the second and third highest monthly searches were in April 2018 and April 2022. The term "Never Gonna Give You Up" was searched most in April 2022, and second most in April 2021. Evidently, it's a common prank to pull on April Fool's Day.
